# Chief Justice attends Pacific Judicial conference hosted in New Zealand

URL: https://pngcje.gov.pg/chief-justice-attends-pacific-judicial-conference-hosted-in-new-zealand/
Date: 2025-02-18T08:54:36
Modified: 2025-02-18T08:54:38

The Chief Justice of Papua New Guinea, Sir Gibbs Salika, attended the 2025 Pacific Judicial Conference (PJC) in New Zealand, accompanied by his team of delegates Justice Nerrie Eliakim, Justice John Carey, Mr. Paul Kelly, Ms. Alison Holt, and Mrs. Stacey Levakia-Wali.

The 2025 Pacific Judicial Conference was hosted in Auckland, New Zealand” from the 9th – 12th February under the theme “Strengthening the Institution of the Judiciary.”

The Pacific Judicial Conference is a high-level judicial conference that is hosted every two to three years and is attended by Chief Justices and Judges from the Pacific Island Nations, as well as other invited judicial guests.

These included the Chief Justices and Judges from, Cook Islands, Niue, Nauru Papua New Guinea, Samoa, Solomon Islands, Guam, New Caledonia, and French Polynesia, among others.

In addition, the Chief Justices of Singapore, Australia, Federal Court of Australia, Australian State Chief Justices, Brunei and Judges from the Ninth Circuit of the United States of America were present.

The host of the conference, Chief Justice of New Zealand RT Hon. Dame Helen Winkelmann, GNZM, in presenting the welcome address, stated that, “All of us who are attending face the challenge of maintaining the strength and legitimacy of the judicial branch of government in a world where the legitimacy of institutions is increasingly up for debate.

Like all institutions, the courts operate in a world of highly polarised opinion – a world dominated by new media, and in which the capabilities of artificial intelligence are beginning to be felt.”

Chief Justice Winklemann further added that, “These are new and evolving challenges for the courts. It will be very interesting to hear how different jurisdictions are meeting these challenges. We have designed the programme to allow a balance between receiving new information and ideas, and having time to share their own experiences”.

The conference programme generated valuable discussions on topics including the protection of judicial independence and the rule of law, the future evolution of courts, the importance of diversity in the judiciary, legal pluralism, and the impact of climate change in the Pacific.
